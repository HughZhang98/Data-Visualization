The relationship between coffee and coding

Coffee production by country in 2020

This is a visual map of the production of coffee exporting countries around the world in 2020 and we can see that most of the coffee exporting countries are near the equator and have a large total export of coffee and a high demand for coffee. Within this Brazil has the largest coffee exports.

Coffee cups per day

This table shows the number of cups of coffee that people drink in a day. We can see that people drink the highest number of 2 cups of coffee per day. In addition, the majority of people drink between 1 and 4 cups of coffee a day.

Gender

This pie chart shows the number of male and female programmers who prefer to drink coffee while typing code, and it is clear that male prefer to drink coffee while typing code, with 74 out of 100 people, or 74% of the total.

Coffee Time

This bar chart shows what time programmers typically drink coffee. We can see that most people will drink coffee while coding. Then there were people who chose to drink coffee before coding. I think it's because people want to relax with the help of coffee.

Coding time and cups for genders

This graph shows the time spent by male and female coding each day and the number of people who choose to drink coffee while coding, we can see that male spend more time coding than female in a day and more male choose to drink coffee while coding than female.

Coding Hours

From this boxplot we can see that the amount of time people coding each day varies, but the median is 7 hours, and according to the graph above most male coding time are greater than 7 hours.
